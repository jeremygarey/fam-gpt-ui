Featured Class: Mastering Civility from the Inside Out

While rewarding, serving in the public sector is often fraught with various pressures, disagreements, and competing positions inherent in our political system. Unsurprisingly, we often witness a counterproductive transformation in others and, if we are honest, in ourselves.

In an instant, our behavior can fluctuate between acceptable productive behavior and out-of-control counterproductive behavior.

Curbing our inclination to act impulsively - to learn to respond rather than react- is primarily a function of self-awareness and self-management.

What happens to cause people you'd never suspect to behave rudely or hurtfully, abruptly, or adopt a win-at-all-cost mentality? The root causes might surprise you.

Learning Objectives:
  • Discover how three distinct aspects of our behavior, assertiveness, incentives, and individuality, shape how we show up productively in our roles
  • Identify the innate expectations you hold in those same three behavior areas that you do not want to be compromised
  • Explore impulsive counterproductive behaviors that emerge when our expectations are unmet and how the lack of self-awareness leads to actions that negatively affect our colleagues and constituents
  • Explore the negative way others will perceive you- and the consequences- when your stress behaviors take over
  • Employ techniques to mitigate the counterproductive stress behaviors

Become grounded in:

  • The productive behaviors you naturally employ
  • The perceptual filters we use to interpret and label others' behavior
  • The sparks that fly (that may not break any rules of conduct) but disregard something even more fundamental: the basic tenets of civility and decorum in our professional relationships

Humility and Self-Management in Leadership

Respect for every individual and leading with humility are the cultural enablers for achieving and sustaining performance excellence. Self-awareness is step one, but self-management is required to build productive relationships and increase your effectiveness.
