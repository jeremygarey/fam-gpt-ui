Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

How do I get started?

Once you register as a student, you will receive a separate email providing a user ID and password, along with a link to the eLearning portal, govxnet.opusworks.com. You may also link to the portal from our website, wintogetherllc.com. Log in to begin!

Why should we use these eLearning modules?

We created the Foundations class for two primary reasons:

  1. To provide an affordable, scalable way for government employees to build capabilities in continuous improvement methods on demand.
  2. Increase the leverage of classroom training as a pre-class assignment, ensuring all students come Day One with a common knowledge base and contextual understanding of how continuous improvement methods apply in the public sector.

What process should I use to share my feedback?

Once you complete each module you will be routed back to the class progress page. The surveys for each module will appear in the activities list. Open the survey for the module you just completed. Respecting your time, you will see only one question on each - a free form response field to capture your improvement suggestions. While we would love to have your feedback so we can continue our own improvement journey, the surveys are optional.

Really, do I have to take tests? This isn't what I signed up for!

Each module includes a post test, and there is a final exam at the conclusion of all five modules; these tests are optional! If you do complete the tests, we will send you the class's electronic badge from the Government Excellence Network™ - a division of Win Together® - for sharing on LinkedIn or other social media platforms. We would love your feedback on the tests. We have built the curriculum with the goal of engaging you to make the learning objectives stick, which should result in your being confident when you take the test. You may re-take the tests as many times as you want to. We analyze the test data regularly to ensure the curriculum itself is meeting the mark.

Is other documentation of class completion available?

Absolutely. You may download your Certificate of Completion in the portal.

Why are you using all of these Japanese words in your training?

The common language of continuous improvement is rooted in Japanese in a similar fashion to Italian words in the language of music. We intentionally chose to include the Japanese words for key concepts to position learners to have a common language for continuing their education and benchmarking with other organizations in their journey to excellence.

What about “belting”?

First of all, many of you are probably wondering "what in the world is belting?" Good question. The use of “belts” to label one's level of training in continuous improvement in believed to have begun in the late 1980's. Analogous to belts in martial arts (i.e., black belt, green/yellow/white belt), the naming convention has become generally accepted terminology to designate one's level of study in continuous improvement methods. We respect the need to benchmark and communicate maturity in the continuous improvement learning journey. It is important to note, there are no universal standards for governance, however. Through our relationship with OpusWorks®, our offerings are founded on the accreditation standards the International Accreditors of Continuing Education and Training (IACET). IACET is an internationally recognized standard development organization that provides a framework for learning process excellence that began as a task force of the US Department of Education in 1968. We will group some of our future offerings in alignment with their training development standards to ensure a high standard of quality and credibility.

How does Foundations in Performance Excellence for Government fit in the belt system?

The Foundations class goes beyond what most organizations consider a White Belt. We anticipate adding additional content in the near future to reach meet/exceed the Yellow Belt requirements. We will offer an upgrade to earn the Yellow Belt certification. Please contact us if you would like to be a beta tester for that program.

I would like to earn my Black Belt. Can I do that through the Government Excellence Network™?

Through our relationship with OpusWorks® and our network of continuous improvement professionals, we plan to offer a government-focused Black Belt certificate program through a blended learning model including eLearning and virtual and/or on-site classroom. Please contact us if you would like to be a beta tester for that offering.

Why did you choose to partner with OpusWorks® Institute?

The OpusWorks® Institute has been providing quality learning solutions since 1992 to accelerate skill building and transform cultures of some of the largest, most respected organizations in the world. To achieve our mission of building cultures of performance excellence at scale in government, we needed a platform that could provide solutions to communities of all sizes and with language translation. Cultural alignment was also an important consideration. OpusWorks® shares our passion to make a meaningful difference in the lives of people.
